FEA of Knit Structures 

OVERVIEW:

For functional wearables, a CAD representation of any knit structures is critical for engineers to communicate the design intent as part of an overall unified system. Further, the analysis and optimization of a design would require an equivalent modulus of these knit structures. We’re proposing here that for engineered textiles, this property should be represented at the stitch level, as opposed to the yarn level (which is too granular) or the bulk material level (which is not granular enough).

  •  Collaboration between engineering and softgoods is critical to the success of functional wearables design.
  •  Making knit technology accessible to designers and engineers is therefore critical to the success of functional wearables that make use of knitting solutions.
  •  Focusing on knits at the stitch level enables the most accessible representation and delivers the most actionable analysis for designers and engineers.
  •  This is a proposal for specific novel techniques for testing and modeling knit structures that enable FEA analysis at the stitch level.

HOW-TO:

STEP ONE:

  •  MODELING SPECIFIC KNIT GEOMETRY AND STRUCTURES
  • START WITH A COLOR-CODED COMPRESSED VERSION OF THE KNIT PROGRAM
  • PROCEDURALLY GENERATE A 3D (BUT FLAT) REPRESENTATION OF THE KNIT PROGRAM
  • REMESH THE FLAT MODEL TO A SHAPED 3D MODEL IF NEEDED, WITH COLOR CODED POLYGONS
  • EXTRUDE THE 3D POLYGON SHELL INTO A SOLID VOXEL MODEL.

STEP TWO:

  •  DESIGN OF EXPERIMENTS TO CAPTURE ALL VARIABLES
  • A SINGLE STITCH-TYPES PROPERTIES ARE DETERMINED BY ITS EIGHT NEAREST NEIGHBORS
  • THE NUMBER OF INDIVIDUAL STITCH TYPES IN A STRUCTURE NEED AN EQUIVALENT NUMBER OF DATAPOINTS TO SOLVE.
